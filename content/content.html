    Bankruptcy - Applications for Form J restrictions

    Where a petition in bankruptcy is filed in the court against a sole registered proprietor, a bankruptcy notice is entered in the register. Once the bankruptcy order has been made against a sole registered proprietor, a bankruptcy restriction is entered in the register.

    A trustee in bankruptcy should not apply for a Form J restriction where:

    • a bankruptcy inhibition or bankruptcy restriction is entered in the register, and
    • the trustee accepts that the registered estate has vested in them.

    This is because, given the existing bankruptcy entry, the entry of the restriction isn't necessary or desirable within s.42(1), LRA 2002. Further, a trustee probably can't rely upon r.93(j), LRR 2003 as they don't have a beneficial interest - a person who is the undivided legal and beneficial owner of property has no separate equitable interest in it (Westdeutsche Landesbank Girozentrale v Islington LBC [1996] AC 669 at 705).

    See Topic - Bankruptcy

    Where one or more of joint registered proprietors is subject to a petition in bankruptcy or bankruptcy order, neither a bankruptcy notice nor a bankruptcy restriction will be entered. However, the Official Receiver or trustee in bankruptcy may apply in form RX1 for a Form J restriction once the bankruptcy order has been made

    • certified copy of the bankruptcy order and
    • •evidence of the trustee in bankruptcy’s appointment.

    The trustee in bankruptcy may apply for a restriction in Form A at the same time and in the same form RX1, as long as a Form A restriction hasn’t already been entered in the register. The bankruptcy will have severed the beneficial joint tenancy
